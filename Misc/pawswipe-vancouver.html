The PawSwipe process

From Stanley Park walk
to forever home

01 🏠
Tell us about your life

Condo in Yaletown or house with a yard in Langley? We match based on your real Vancouver lifestyle — not just what you think you want.

02 ✨
Swipe through local pets

Browse cards pulled live from BC SPCA, VOKRA, RAPS, LAPS, and the City of Vancouver shelter. Every pet is real and available today.

03 💬
Connect with the shelter

Message BC SPCA directly, book a meet-and-greet at 1205 E 7th Ave, or schedule a VOKRA foster visit — all inside the app.

04 🎉
Bring them home

All BC SPCA adoptions include microchip + BC Pet Registry lifetime registration. We walk you through every step of the paperwork.

✓ Official Rescue Authority
BC SPCA — Vancouver & West Vancouver
📍 1205 E 7th Ave, Vancouver · 1020 Pound Rd, West Vancouver

The BC SPCA is the only animal welfare organization in BC with authority to enforce animal cruelty laws. Their Vancouver branch serves the Lower Mainland with two facilities, a non-profit vet hospital, and 36 branches province-wide. All adoptions include microchip + lifetime BC Pet Registry registration. Over 53,000 animals helped annually across BC.

VOKRA
📍 2028 Wall St, Vancouver BC · (778) 874-1486

Vancouver Orphan Kitten Rescue Association has been finding forever homes for cats and kittens since 2000. Entirely volunteer-driven with 550+ foster homes across Metro Vancouver. No-kill policy. Over 1,400 cats adopted annually. All cats live in foster homes — never a cold shelter environment.

RAPS — Regional Animal Protection Society
📍 Richmond, BC · rapsbc.com

A 27-year-old no-kill organization that operates the largest cat sanctuary in North America (370+ unadoptable cats cared for permanently), plus an adoption centre, non-profit RAPS Animal Hospital, and three thrift stores. Open for adoptions Sat & Sun 12–4pm.

LAPS — Langley Animal Protection Society
📍 Patti Dale Animal Shelter, Langley · lapsbc.ca

LAPS serves both the City and Township of Langley, expanded in 2026 with new Animal Control Officers and enhanced sheltering. Runs the unique Doghouse program with Correctional Services Canada. Foster-based care minimizes shelter stress. Adoptable dogs and cats listed at lapsbc.ca.
